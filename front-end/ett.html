Stress Tests

Stress tests evaluate your heart’s response to exercise or medication-induced stress.

At Burnaby Hospital, these include:
  • Exercise/Treadmill Stress Test
  • Exercise Myocardial Perfusion Imaging (MIBI)
  • Persantine MIBI Stress Test
  • Dobutamine MIBI Stress test

What to Expect

The test takes 30–45 minutes

For all stress tests, 10 stickers (electrodes) will be placed on your chest.
  • Clothing from the waist up will need to be removed to allow for electrode placement.
    • You will be provided with a patient gown.
  • If needed, small areas of body hair may be shaved to ensure proper electrode contact.

All tests are safe, minimally invasive, monitored by the technologist(s), and supervised by the clinic physician.

Exercise Myocardial Perfusion Imaging (MIBI)

Essentially an Exercise/Treadmill Stress Test with an imaging component.

  • You will be walking/jogging on a treadmill that gets faster and steeper every 3 minutes.
  • Once target heart rate is reached, a small amount of radioactive tracer is injected into your vein.
    • If you are unable to get to target heart rate, you will do a different kind of stress test instead if appropriate.
  • You will need to continue walking/jogging on the treadmill one minute after injection.
  • After a brief recovery period, you will go to the Nuclear Medicine department to take pictures of your heart.


Persantine MIBI Stress Test

The persantine medication simulates exercise in patients who are unable to physically exercise on a treadmill.

Persantine increases blood flow to the heart by dilating (widening) the coronary arteries.
  • If you have asthma or other respiratory conditions, please inform the technologist.
  • The persantine test will not work if you've had caffeine within 24 hours prior to the test. This includes:
    • Coffee
    • Tea
    • Energy drinks
    • Chocolate
  • You will be lying down throughout the entire test.
  • The persantine medication is administered through an IV line over 4 minutes.
  • Possible side effects include:
    • Headache
    • Chest discomfort
    • Flushing (warmth and redness in the face)
  • Shortly after the medication is given, a small amount of radioactive tracer is injected into your vein.
  • After a brief recovery period to ensure any side effects are gone, you will go to the Nuclear Medicine department to take pictures of your heart.

Dobutamine MIBI Stress Test

The Dobutamine stress test is performed when an exercise or persantine stress test cannot be done.

Dobutamine works by stimulating the heart to beat faster and more strongly, similar to the effects of exercise.
  • Please inform the technologist if you have a history of heart rhythm abnormalities.
  • You will be lying down throughout the entire test.
  • The Dobutamine medication is administered gradually through an IV line.
  • Your heart rate, blood pressure, and ECG will be continuously monitored.
  • Possible side effects include:
    • Palpitations (feeling your heart beat faster)
    • Chest discomfort
    • Headache
    • Nausea
  • Once target heart rate is reached, a small amount of radioactive tracer is injected into your vein.
  • If the target heart rate is not achieved, then the clinic doctor may choose to administer atropine to help increase your heart rate.
  • After a brief recovery period to ensure any side effects have resolved, you will go to the Nuclear Medicine department to take pictures of your heart.
